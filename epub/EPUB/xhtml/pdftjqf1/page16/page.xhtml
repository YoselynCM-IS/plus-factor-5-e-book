Identify main ideas, introductory and supporting sentences

 

The main idea is usually the first sentence of the paragraph, but it can also be an introductory

 

sentence, so we have to identify if it is the main idea or an introduction to the topic. The

 

rest of the paragraph supports the main idea. You must remember that every paragraph

 

has a main idea. For example, check this paragraph:

 

The movie Apollo 13 was a hit for the summer of 1995. It is

 

an exciting story about space exploration. In the movie, the

 

astronauts get in trouble while they are trying to return to Earth.

 

People in the audience are on the edge of their seats waiting to

 

see what happens.

 

In this paragraph, the topic is the movie Apollo 13, and the main idea is in the first sentence:

 

Apollo 13 was a hit for the summer of 1995.

 

Another example:

 

Does watching violence on TV make people more prone to violence themselves? 

 

Obviously, TV violence has some negative effects. One study found that heavy TV

 

watchers are more fearful than others. They always try to protect themselves.

 

In this paragraph, the first sentence is an introductory sentence. The second sentence, is

 

the main idea.
