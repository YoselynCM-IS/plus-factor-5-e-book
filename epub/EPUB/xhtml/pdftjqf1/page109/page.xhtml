I.
Read these stories of successful people.

 

Dikembe Mutombo 
, one of the greatest NBA players was born in 1966

 

in Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of Congo. He speaks English, French,

 

Spanish, Portuguese, and five Central African varieties, including Lingala

 

and Tshiluba. He is a member of the Luba ethnic group.

 

He has helped his hometown in many ways, but the biggest one is the

 

Biamba Marie Mutombo Hospital he built in memory of his mother, who

 

died because she couldn’t get medical help.

 

“If you wanna do something, if you wanna make your change, make

 

sure that is coming from the bottom of your heart. Make sure your heart

 

is speaking to you from inside, that you want to commit with yourself for the rest of your

 

life.” Dikembe has been raising funds all over the world.

 

“I have been blessed to have the opportunity to make a good living playing the

 

game of basketball and I feel it is my duty to help give back to those who were less

 

fortunate,” Mutombo said. "I believe there are many other athletes that feel like I do

 

and I think it is great that they are beginning to receive credit for their efforts.”

 

There is a proverb in Africa, “When you take the elevator to go up, you always must

 

remember to send it back down. This is my way of sending it back down.”

 

When  
Afroz Shah 
, a young Indian lawyer from Mumbai, came back

 

to his town, the city of Versova, he was shocked by the tons of trash

 

on the beautiful beach he had left some years ago. Determined to do

 

something about it, he started cleaning up the beach with a friend,

 

one piece of rubbish at a time.

 

Shah knew they couldn’t do it alone, so he knocked every door in

 

Versova explaining to the neighbors how trash was damaging the

 

ocean. He went to schools asking for help, and nowadays, the volunteers have collected

 

over 4,000 tons of trash from the 2.5-kilometer beach.

 

“We can’t leave the government in  
isolation. Whoever is in power, whichever  
party 
, if

 

the party is implementing something, we’ll have to hold their hand. I would suggest to

 

love your country, love your nature. There’s no love among citizens any more. There is

 

more hate in this world than love. I think Mother Nature can bond all of us”.

 

Shah is very proud of the Versova residents' accomplishments. This movement

 

brought marine life to the attention of the government, and it is also starting to win

 

back the beach, with decreasing amounts of new litter appearing each month.
