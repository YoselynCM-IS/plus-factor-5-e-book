WRITING:

 

Function of supporting sentences

 

A text is any piece of writing. This could be a letter, an email, a novel, a poem, a recipe, a

 

note, instructions on how to “do it yourself”, an article in a newspaper or magazine, writing

 

on a webpage or an advert. All of these examples can be called texts.

 

An
informative 
text is a text that wants to advise or tell you about something. It usually:

 

avoids repetition, contains facts, gives information in a clear way; introducing the subject

 

and then developing it, for example:

 

The Birth of an Island

 

M

 

illions of years ago, a volcano built a mountain on the

 

floor of the Atlantic. Eruption after eruption, it pushed

 

up a lot of volcanic rock, until it accumulated a big mass and

 

reached the surface of the sea. Finally, its cone emerged as

 

an island with an area of 200 square miles. Eventually, the

 

waves of the Atlantic cut down the cone and reduced it to a

 

shoal which remained above water. This is the fragment we

 

know as Bermuda.

 

The life story of Bermuda has been repeated by almost every one of the islands

 

that interrupt the flow of water in the oceans. These isolated islands in the sea are

 

different from the continents. The major land masses have not changed throughout

 

time. But islands are ephemeral, created today, and destroyed tomorrow. With few

 

exceptions, they are the result of the violent, explosive, earth-shaking eruptions of

 

submarine volcanoes.

 

The birth of a volcanic island is an event marked by prolonged and violent

 

movements: the forces of the earth struggle to create new land. The sea floor, where

 

an island begins, has deep cracks and fissures, along which the molten lava presses

 

up and finally bursts forth into the sea. But a submarine volcano is different from a

 

terrestrial eruption, where the lava, molten rocks and gases are thrown into the air

 

through an open crater. Here on the bottom of the ocean the volcano has to resist all

 

the weight of the ocean water above it.

 

Adapted from  
The Sea Around Us  
by Rachel Carson.
