grammar/third- 

 

conditional

 

The  
third conditional  
structure is used to talk about  
impossible 

 

conditions 
which happened in the past and which  
cannot be changed.

 

Formula: If + past perfect, would / could / might + have + past participle.

 

•
If I had studied, I would have passed. (I didn’t study I didn’t pass.)

 

•
I would have phoned her if she had given me her phone number.  

 

(She didn’t give me her phone number I didn’t call her.)

 

•
The third conditional is also used to express regrets, give criticism, and

 

wish for changes in the past. We can use could / might instead of would.

 

•
If I had apologized, we might have fixed our relationship.

 

•
I could have bought the gift if I had saved enough money.
