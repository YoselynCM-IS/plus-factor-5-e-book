A myth is a traditional story intended to explain some mystery of nature, religious doctrine,

 

or cultural belief.

 

I.
Check these myths. Have you heard about any of them?

 

Atlantis is an island described by Plato as an

 

advanced civilization with unknown technology

 

which disappeared because they challenged the

 

gods. A theory says it was destroyed because of

 

an experiment with nuclear energy. Some others

 

say it disappeared because of a big tsunami.

 

Made up of several continents, oceans, mountains and

 

rivers, Shambhala is its central city. There would be about

 

a hundred underground colonies beneath the Earth, all

 

but one quite close to the surface. These underground

 

cities have been known as the Agartha Network.

 

Telos is a very advanced city placed under Mount

 

Shasta in California. People there live in peace and

 

harmony with nature and expect the right moment to

 

share all their technology with humans

 

Lemuria, a lost continent in the Pacific Ocean which

 

sunk, and the only thing we can see today are the

 

islands of Hawaii as the remains of this great civilization.

 

II.  
Work in groups of three and talk about these myths and try to find more

 

information about them.

 

III.  
Write about one of them in your notebook and share it with your classmates.
