The Meaning of Happiness

 

For thousands of years, people have been debating

 

to find the meaning of happiness and, nowadays,

 

we have different points of view and theories. For

 

example, some people say happiness doesn’t mean

 

feeling happy all the time, but enjoying everything

 

we experience in our everyday life.

 

In her 2007 book  
The How of Happiness 
, Sonja Lyubomirsky defines a happy

 

person as someone who frequently experiences joy, contentment, or positive well-

 

being, combined with a sense that one’s life is good, meaningful, and worthwhile.

 

Some others say that our happiness is a combination of how satisfied we feel with

 

our life, how good we feel on a day-to-day basis, and how engaged we are with our

 

family and friends.

 

There is also the most common theory which talks about happiness as having a

 

lot of money to buy whatever we want, on the other hand, some people think money

 

can’t buy you happiness but it sure makes life easier.

 

When we look at the whole picture, being happy is feeling satisfaction and

 

having meaning in life. It is having the ability not only to feel positive emotions but

 

to overcome negative and sad emotions. Being happy gives us the ability to connect

 

with others, enjoy with others and share with others.
