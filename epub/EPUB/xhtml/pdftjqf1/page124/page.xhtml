Unit 1

MODALS

Deduction (present) modal 
+
infinitive.

Must: 
when we are sure or there’s strong evidence of something.

May, 
Might, 
Could: 
when something is possible or we are not sure.

Can’t: 
when we are sure something is not true.

Deduction (past) modal 
+
perfect infinitive.

Must + perfect infinitive: 
when we are sure about something in the past.

May, Might, Could:  
when something was possible, but we are not sure.

Can’t + perfect infinitive: 
when we are sure something in the past didn’t happen.

Obligation and necessity

Must 
expresses internal obligation. The speaker feels that something must be done.

Have to 
expresses external obligation, which comes from external factors, and not from the speaker’s

feelings.

IMPERATIVE SENTENCES

An imperative sentence gives a command. It can end in a full stop or an exclamation mark.

Clean 
this room by tomorrow!  
Please  
tidy 
your room.

PAST PERFECT

Is used to talk about an action taking place before a certain time in the past.

Affirmative: I had spoken.  
 
Negative: I had not spoken.  
 
Interrogative: Had I spoken?
