Emotions and feelings

 

Making the most basic difference between emotions

 

and feelings is not easy. It is said that we feel our emo-

 

tions, but we may not necessarily emote our feelings.

 

I can show my emotions through words, facial

 

expressions, or body language, but maybe I do not

 

show my feelings. It is so, because feelings are deeper

 

than emotions.

 

Emotions are physical unconscious signals that

 

our body is constantly giving us. These reactions

 

are instantaneous, for example, if we feel joyful, we

 

immediately may start shouting, laughing, or even singing. But, once the cause of joyfulness

 

has gone, we will be perfectly calm. For this reason, we can say that emotions come and go.

 

Now, feelings are formed by emotions. It is different for everyone and while two people

 

may experience the same emotions, their feelings may be different. This is due to the fact

 

that personal experiences affect the way we take the emotions. We can say that emotions

 

are superficial and feelings are much stronger and deeper than emotions. The secret lies

 

in managing our emotions as they are temporary experiences, but our feelings support

 

our identity.
